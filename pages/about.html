The Evolution of a Trader-Engineer

This is not just a bio. It is a documentation of failure, learning, and the relentless pursuit of logical perfection.

Chapter 1: The Initial Drawdown

Every trader has a story that begins with a loss. Mine is no different. My journey into the financial markets began with enthusiasm, fueled by the bull runs and the promise of quick capital. I dove headfirst into trading without a parachute—no risk management, no psychological discipline, and no system.

I lost. I saw my portfolio bleed red. I watched as the markets moved against my positions with ruthless efficiency. It was a period of intense frustration. I was a Computer Science student who understood logic, yet I was failing in a marketplace driven by emotion.

But in engineering, a system crash is not the end; it is an opportunity to debug. I realized that I wasn't losing because the market was rigged; I was losing because my code—my internal logic—was flawed. I needed to rewrite my approach from the kernel up.

LESSON LEARNED:

"The market does not care about your feelings. It only respects your discipline."

The Pivot: Instead of chasing pumps, I started studying charts like I study code. I began looking for variables, constants, and loops in price action. I realized that a successful trade is just a function of correct analysis and strict execution parameters.
